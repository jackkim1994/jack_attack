About Me

Origin

I was born and raised in Hong Kong, a renowned tourist spot and financial hub in East Asia.
I was fortunate to visit different cities and explore cultures at an early age, developing my ability to accept different perspectives and adjust accordingly.
Even though I am Korean, my international background has often led me to call myself a global nomad.

Military Service
In the middle of my undergraduate year at UCLA, I joined the two-year military service at the Republic of Korea Air Force.
It was an intriguing experience that gave me an insight into how the "company" operated, although it was stricter and operated at a smaller scale.
I learned the importance of responsibility, discipline, and proactiveness, which became my foundation when I returned to the United States.

What am I doing now?

I am currently studying Machine Learning and Data Science as a graduate student at Northwestern University.
Previously, I worked as a Data Analyst at GOBankingRates for around four years.
In this company, I encountered a banking client that implemented an imperfect fraud detection algorithm that hurt marketing performances across partner affiliates, including us.
After experiencing this incident, I became more interested in data science and desired to contribute.
While I am still determining where I will end up in my full-time career after completing the graduate program, I am willing to continue honing my skills in the ever-growing data science field.
